Hey, I'm Brandon!

Father of four amazing kids, tech enthusiast, and proud Xennial from Holden, Missouri.

About Me

I was born in 1977 and grew up with technology as part of my DNA. I'm what they call a Xennial—that micro-generation caught between Gen X and Millennials. While some of my peers lean toward Gen X traits, I've always been fascinated by technology, which pushed me more toward the Millennial side of things.

Technology isn't just what I do; it's who I am. I grew up in an era when computers were obsolete the day after you bought them. I watched Apple fire Steve Jobs, nearly collapse, bring him back, and then become the most successful tech company in history. Sometimes I think about going back to 1994, dropping out of high school, working three jobs, and investing everything into Apple stock. I'd be a multi-millionaire today—if only I'd known!

Learning new technologies and adapting to the ever-changing tech landscape is second nature to me. What I consider trivial, others sometimes assume I know nothing about. That's the beauty and challenge of living in a world where technology never stops evolving.

What I Do

Hobbies & Interests

Cooking

I'm serious about my barbecue and grilling. I've mastered the art of charcoal cooking—brisket, pulled pork, ribs (beef and pork), smoked sausages, chicken, turkey, wings, meatloaf, you name it. I've also perfected the art of steak craft and can consistently produce a perfectly cooked steak. Whether it's on the smoker, the grill, or in the kitchen, I love creating delicious meals.

Shooting

I qualified as a marksman three times during my active duty in the Air Force, and I still enjoy shooting for target practice. It's a skill I'm proud of and a hobby I find relaxing.

Fishing

Who doesn't love fishing? I'm pretty good at it, but honestly, it's more about the enjoyment than flexing any skills. There's something peaceful about being out on the water.

Mountain Biking

This is an old passion I haven't pursued in a while since I'm not in the right shape for it. But I've mountain biked in Colorado, Kansas, Missouri, Nebraska, Iowa, New York, Connecticut, and even Korea. Someday I'll get back out there.

I own a set of weights and fitness equipment that I use every now and then (though not as often as I should!). When I was younger, I was really into weight lifting and found the fittest time of my life. It's something I'd like to get back into more seriously.

Racquetball

Racquetball is the most fun and healthy way to do cardio, in my opinion. I enjoy playing, but I prefer it casual—when it gets ultra-competitive, I'd rather find another partner or just play by myself.

I've been making home brew on and off for over 20 years. Most of my brews have been successful, though there have been a few learning experiences that unfortunately got dumped out. Now I can make a very good brew quite consistently. I wish I had the time and the right setup to keep doing it—it's been a while.

Video Games

This was more of a childhood passion that I enjoyed until I decided to pursue game development as a career. I'm not sure if learning the behind-the-scenes disenchanted video games for me, or if starting a family shifted my priorities.
